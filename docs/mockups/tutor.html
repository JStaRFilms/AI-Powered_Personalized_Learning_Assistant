Absolutely. The official definition is dense.

Think of a horizontal asymptote not as a strict barrier, but as a "long-term trend."

If you look at the function f(x) = 1/x, as x gets massively huge (like 1,000,000), the fraction becomes incredibly tiny (0.000001). It gets closer and closer to zero, but never actually hits exactly zero.

That "trend toward zero" as you move indefinitely to the right is the horizontal asymptote.
